Why Semantic Tokens?

Descriptive design language works better for AI than raw CSS values. Semantic tokens encode purpose, not just pixels.

When AI generates code, raw hex codes have no meaning. A color like #1a365d doesn't tell you whether it's a background, text, or border color. Semantic tokens solve this by naming the role, not just the value.

The problem with raw values

Most design tokens in codebases are flat — they describe what something looks like but not what it does. When an AI model encounters #1a365d, it has no way to know:

Is this a background color? A text color? A border? A hover state? An accent? The AI has to guess, and guessing leads to inconsistent output.

Semantic naming

With semantic tokens, every value carries context. Compare:

#171f33 → just a dark blue-gray

surface-container (#171f33) → a container surface, sits above the base surface, pairs with on-surface for text

The name encodes purpose, relationship, and usage. An AI can confidently assign surface-container to a card or sidebar background because the name tells it that's what the token is for.

Benefits for AI generation

BenefitWhy it matters
ConsistencySame token → same value everywhere → AI chooses the right token every time instead of inventing a new hex code
IntentToken names encode purpose → AI understands layout hierarchy (surface vs. container vs. accent)
MaintainabilityChange one token → updates everywhere → AI can refactor confidently without hunting for values
Cross-formatSame tokens → Tailwind/CSS/DTCG → AI works in any framework without translation errors

Example: before and after

Here's what happens when you give an AI raw values versus semantic tokens:

BEFORE — Raw values
"A card with #1a365d background and #dae2fd text"
AFTER — Semantic tokens
"A card with surface-container background and on-surface text"

The semantic version is clearer, more maintainable, and produces better results because the AI understands the design intent — not just pixel values.

Key insight

When you use semantic tokens, the AI understands your design intent, not just pixel values. This means fewer hallucinations, more consistent output, and designs that actually follow your system.
